# Writing Style Guidelines

Content should be direct, audience-first, and precise. Every word earns its place.

## Tone of Voice — Four Pillars

1. **Direct and unambiguous.** Every word has a job. If it doesn't earn its place, cut it. Your target audience is busy. The copy respects their time. Short sentences. Max 25 words per sentence.
2. **Technically precise.** When talking about technology, be exact. No euphemisms, no overselling. For audience-facing blog content, translate tech into outcomes — but don't shy away from precise terms when they add clarity.
3. **Audience-first, not feature-first.** We don't sell features. We sell what those features do for the reader's business. Lead with outcomes, then explain the how.
4. **Aspirational without grandiosity.** We have ambition. We express it through facts, not promises. Use specific numbers, brand examples, or scenarios.

## Additional Style Rules

- **Conversational.** Use contractions, direct address ("you", "your business"), and short paragraphs.
- **Active voice.** Not passive constructions.
- **Concrete over abstract.** Replace vague claims with specific outcomes.

## What to Avoid

- Filler phrases: "In today's competitive landscape", "It goes without saying", "At the end of the day"
- Over-qualification: "It might potentially help to consider" — just say "Do this"
- Walls of text: No paragraph longer than 3-4 sentences. In JSON body/description fields, separate paragraphs with `\n`. Never have more than 5-6 lines of text without a line break
- Grandiose claims: "revolutionize", "game-changing", "disruptive" — show, don't declare
